Data notes & caveats

Committees, APPGs and frontbench teams are anchor nodes; parliamentarians connect to the bodies they belong to. Dotted person-to-person ties show shared public engagement with a defence issue. Every node records its source and date.

  • Defence Committee membership is from committees.parliament.uk. Calvin Bailey MP still appears on the published committee list and as registered Chair of APPG Armed Forces Community, but was appointed Minister for Veterans and People in June 2026 and relinquishes both by convention; he is mapped through the ministerial team only, and the APPG chair is treated as vacant pending the next register edition.
  • Business and Trade Committee membership is from committees.parliament.uk (accessed 7 Jul 2026). Its Sub-Committee on Economic Security, Arms and Export Controls is shown with its Chair only.
  • Ministerial and shadow posts per GOV.UK (3 Jul 2026) and members.parliament.uk, reflecting the 11 June 2026 reshuffle. Dr Kieran Mullan is shown on the Shadow Defence Team per his opening of the 23 June Opposition Day debate; exact portfolio title awaits confirmation.
  • APPG data shows officers only (register editions 23 Feb and 18 May 2026); full memberships are not centrally published. Register entries can pre-date the June 2026 reshuffle.
  • Topic ties (purple dashed lines) connect MPs curated as active on the same defence topic — Combat Air, Maritime & Submarines, Nuclear Deterrent, Rotary Wing, Land Systems & Munitions, Personnel & Veterans, Industrial Base, Export Controls & Economic Security, NATO & Alliance Policy. Each tag on each person carries individually cited evidence (debate, date, link, or role). This layer is deliberately interpretive: I judged the topic assignment; the evidence beneath each tag is the objective part. Where citable evidence was thin, people were left untagged rather than assigned a topic on inference. Ministers and shadows are tagged only when the portfolio or a specific contribution makes the topic explicit.
  • Interest signals (dotted ties) connect MPs who spoke on, or publicly engaged with, the same defence topic: the Defence Investment Plan (Jun–Jul 2026), the Typhoon Sovereign Capability debate (12 Nov 2025), and the SDR Funding debate (15 Apr 2026). Each member carries an individual citation; two MPs joined by a dotted line may never have spoken to each other. Ministers and frontbenchers responding ex officio are excluded — departmental duty is not an interest signal. Coverage is limited to debates verified in compiling this map, not an exhaustive Hansard cross-reference of all members; unnamed contributors to those debates are omitted rather than inferred, except where flagged. Dr Kieran Mullan is shown on the Shadow Defence Team per his opening of the 23 June Opposition Day debate; his exact portfolio title awaits confirmation on members.parliament.uk.
  • PPS coverage: Ben Obese-Jecty is shown as Shadow PPS (Defence) per contemporaneous reporting of his June 2025 appointment; no change has been reported since. On the government side, both defence PPSs resigned on 11 June 2026 and no successors have been published (the last GOV.UK PPS list is November 2025 and is out of date) — government defence PPS slots are shown as vacant rather than populated from stale lists. Pamela Nash and Rachel Hopkins appear as former PPSs within the DIP engagement cohort.
  • Defence-Industrial Constituency Champions is an analytical grouping, not a parliamentary body. Inclusion requires both a major defence-industrial site in or adjacent to the constituency and a cited defence contribution in Parliament within the past 18 months (Hansard, accessed via TheyWorkForYou where noted). It is a verified starter set, not a census: many qualifying constituencies (e.g. Stevenage/MBDA, Brough, Govan and Scotstoun, Rosyth) await verification and can be added as citations are confirmed. Two links carry attribution-by-constituency flags (Stockport, Templecombe).
  • NATO Parliamentary Assembly delegation is as announced by written ministerial statement on 5 November 2024 (parliament.uk, accessed 8 July 2026). Parliament notes former Members continue to serve until the Chief Whips appoint a new delegation. Derek Twigg leads; Lord Spellar and Emma Lewell also hold other mapped roles.
  • Practitioner-identified interest: a small number of MPs (currently Paul Holmes) are included on the basis of constituency defence-industrial footprint and practitioner knowledge rather than a formal defence role on the parliamentary record; these carry an explicit corroboration flag and should not be presented as holding defence positions.
  • Registered group funders are excluded from this version by design. Manufacturing, Apprenticeships and Skills APPGs are excluded pending officer verification; no register entity exists for "economic growth".
  • Party grouping tags (ERG, Blue Labour, One Nation) are marked "reported affiliation"; items badged amber derive from general knowledge and should be confirmed before external circulation.
  • Ministerial correspondence addresses for the five MoD ministers use the official departmental route published in the Cabinet Office's List of Ministerial Responsibilities (ParliBranch-MinCorrespondence@mod.gov.uk) — mail addressed to a named minister at this address reaches their private office. This is shown alongside, not instead of, their personal parliamentary email, since both are legitimate contact routes.
  • Parliamentary email addresses are generated from Parliament's own standard constituent-contact format (firstname.lastname.mp@parliament.uk) rather than scraped from any directory — this is genuinely public information Parliament itself publishes as the way to reach any MP. Hyphenated or compound names are flagged "verify pattern" since the standard format occasionally varies for these. Members of the House of Lords do not use this format and are noted separately with the general Lords contact route instead of a guessed address.
